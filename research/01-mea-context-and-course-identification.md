# MEA context, course identification and branding

## School identity

- Manchester Enterprise Academy (MEA)
- Simonsway, Wythenshawe, Manchester, M22 9RH
- Part of Prospere Learning Trust
- Published vision: **“A Fantastic Future for All.”**
- The academic curriculum aims for students to know more and remember more.
- MEA’s published principles include: factual knowledge before skill; memory as the residue of thought; deep knowledge built from shallow knowledge; proficiency through practice; and improvement through sustained hard work.

These principles point to a site built around explicit knowledge maps, retrieval, repeated practice, progress visibility and encouraging effort-based feedback.

## Qualification identification

### Business

MEA’s maps use Pearson Theme 1 “Investigating small business” and Theme 2 “Building a business,” which uniquely match Pearson Edexcel GCSE Business **1BS0**.

### Enterprise

MEA’s maps use R067, R068 and R069, matching OCR Level 1/Level 2 Cambridge National in Enterprise and Marketing **J837**.

### Computer Science

MEA’s maps use sections 1.1–1.6 and 2.1–2.5 and explicitly reference OCR, matching OCR GCSE Computer Science **J277**.

### iMedia

MEA’s maps use R093, R094 and R095 and explicitly describe comic creation, matching OCR Level 1/Level 2 Cambridge National in Creative iMedia **J834**, with optional unit **R095 Characters and comics**.

## MEA curriculum sequence

### Pearson Business

Year 10:

- Autumn 1: 1.1 Enterprise and entrepreneurship; 1.2 Spotting a business opportunity; start 1.3 Putting a business idea into practice.
- Autumn 2: continue 1.3.
- Spring 1: 1.4 Making the business effective.
- Spring 2: finish 1.4 and begin 1.5 Understanding external influences.
- Summer 1: complete 1.5.
- Summer 2: start Theme 2 and develop exam technique.

Year 11:

- Autumn: 2.1 Growing the business, 2.2 Making marketing decisions, then 2.3 Making operational decisions, with Theme 1 recap.
- Spring: 2.4 Making financial decisions and 2.5 Making human resource decisions, then revision.
- Summer: final revision and examination preparation.
- The maps reference regular mini-tests and 60-minute full-paper-style/progress assessments.

### OCR Computer Science

Year 10 covers the whole specification:

- Autumn: 1.1 Systems architecture; 1.2 Memory and storage; 1.3 Networks; 1.4 Network security.
- Spring: 1.5 Systems software; 1.6 Impacts; 2.1 Algorithms; 2.2 Programming fundamentals.
- Summer: complete 2.2, then 2.3 Robust programs, 2.4 Boolean logic and 2.5 Languages/IDEs.

Year 11 concentrates on retrieval, harder exam questions, alternating Paper 1 and Paper 2 coverage, extended responses and programming/reference-language practice.

### OCR Enterprise and Marketing

Year 10:

- R068 Design a business proposal is developed through autumn and spring.
- R069 begins in summer with brand development and the promotional campaign.

Year 11:

- The first two pages of the published map refer to expected enterprise skills: brand, campaign, pitch and R067 concepts.
- Pages 3–5 unexpectedly switch to Pearson Business Theme 2 content. This appears to be a publication/copying error rather than a genuine Enterprise sequence.
- Do not expose an inferred week-by-week Year 11 Enterprise plan until the subject lead confirms it.

### OCR Creative iMedia

Year 10:

- R094 Visual identity and digital graphics, using Photoshop.
- R093 Creative iMedia in the media industry fundamentals.
- R095 Characters and comics begins in Summer 2.

Year 11:

- Autumn: complete R095.
- Spring and summer: R093 knowledge and exam preparation.
