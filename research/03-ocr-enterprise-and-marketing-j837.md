# OCR Level 1/Level 2 Cambridge National in Enterprise and Marketing — J837

## Qualification at a glance

- Technical Award available in England; included on Key Stage 4 performance tables.
- 120 guided learning hours; 200 total UMS.
- All three units are mandatory.
- R067 Enterprise and marketing concepts: 48 GLH, 70 raw marks/80 UMS, 40%.
- R068 Design a business proposal: 36 GLH, 60 marks/UMS, 30%.
- R069 Market and pitch a business proposal: 36 GLH, 60 marks/UMS, 30%.
- Grades: Level 2 Distinction*, Distinction, Merit, Pass; Level 1 Distinction, Merit, Pass; Unclassified.
- Overall fixed UMS boundaries out of 200: D*2 180; D2 160; M2 140; P2 120; D1 100; M1 80; P1 60.
- Performance-table points are not direct GCSE grade equivalences and should not be labelled as such.

## Terminal assessment rule

R067 must be taken in the student’s final assessment series for the qualification. R068 and R069 must be submitted before or in that same series. If a student took R067 earlier, that result becomes practice once the terminal exam is sat. A qualification retake requires the exam to be retaken to satisfy the terminal rule again.

This needs an explainer in the eventual planner because a student cannot simply keep an earlier high R067 result while resubmitting later NEA in a new certification attempt.

## R067 — Enterprise and marketing concepts

### Assessment format

- 1 hour 15 minutes.
- 70 marks; calculator permitted.
- Part A: 10 multiple-choice questions, 10 marks.
- Part B: 60 marks based on one developing enterprise context.
- Part B uses short and medium responses plus one eight-mark level-of-response question linked to Topic Area 4.
- Students need recall, application to the named enterprise, numerical work, connected analysis and a context-based extended judgement.

### Topic Area 1 — Characteristics, risk and reward for enterprise

#### Entrepreneur characteristics

- Creativity and innovation.
- Risk taking.
- Communication.
- Negotiation.
- Confidence.
- Determination.

Students must explain why a characteristic matters in a specific situation, not merely give a dictionary definition.

#### Rewards of risk taking

- Financial reward.
- Independence.
- Self-satisfaction.
- Making a change or pursuing a personal idea.

#### Drawbacks of risk taking

- Financial loss or insecurity.
- Effects on wellbeing.
- Work-life imbalance and time demands.
- Personal/reputational consequences.

### Topic Area 2 — Market research to target a specific customer

#### Purpose

- Understand the market and customer.
- Reduce risk.
- Inform product, price, promotion and selling decisions.
- Identify gaps, demand and competitors.

#### Primary research

- Questionnaires/surveys.
- Interviews.
- Focus groups.
- Observation.
- Trialling/testing where relevant.

Students must distinguish the method from a delivery channel: an “online questionnaire” is still a questionnaire; “online” alone is not the method.

#### Secondary research

- Internal data.
- Government publications/statistics.
- Market reports.
- Competitor websites and published information.
- Internet, newspapers, trade publications and other credible existing sources.

#### Data

- Quantitative and qualitative data.
- Benefits and limitations of both.
- Reliability, relevance, currency, sample and bias.

#### Market segmentation

- Age.
- Gender.
- Occupation.
- Income.
- Geographic location.
- Lifestyle.

Students must connect the segment to a real decision, such as product features, price, channel or promotion.

### Topic Area 3 — What makes a product financially viable

- Fixed costs and variable costs.
- Total cost and cost per unit.
- Sales revenue.
- Profit and loss.
- Break-even point and break-even graphs.
- Using break-even to make decisions; do not confuse this with defining break-even.
- Difference between cash and profit.
- Why a profitable enterprise can still fail through lack of cash.

Core formulae:

- `total costs = fixed costs + variable costs`
- `total revenue = selling price × number sold`
- `profit/loss = total revenue − total costs`
- `break-even output = fixed costs ÷ contribution per unit`
- `contribution per unit = selling price − variable cost per unit`

### Topic Area 4 — Creating a marketing mix to support a product

#### The mix

- Product, price, place and promotion.
- Interdependence: a decision about one element affects the others.
- Appropriateness for product, target customer, objective, budget and competition.

#### Promotion and communication

- Non-digital advertising such as print, outdoor and broadcast.
- Digital advertising such as websites, social media, email, search and online video/connected television.
- Sales promotion methods.
- Public relations.
- Strengths, weaknesses, reach, targeting, cost and suitability.

#### Selling and distribution

- Physical selling locations/channels.
- Digital/online selling.
- Choosing a channel for the target market and product.

#### Product life cycle

- Development, introduction, growth, maturity and decline.
- Extension strategies and why they may delay decline.

#### Pricing

- Factors influencing price.
- Pricing strategies and their suitability for objectives, costs, competitors and customers.

### Topic Area 5 — Factors to consider when starting up and running an enterprise

#### Ownership

- Sole trader.
- Partnership.
- Limited liability partnership.
- Private limited company.
- Franchise.
- Control, liability, finance, decision-making, profit sharing and continuity.

#### Sources of capital

- Personal savings.
- Family/friends.
- Bank loan.
- Crowdfunding.
- Grants.
- Business angels.

#### Support

- Banks.
- Local councils.
- Accountants and solicitors.
- Family/friends.
- Chambers of commerce/business networks.
- Government and charities/enterprise organisations.

Students must explain the type of help and why it fits the enterprise’s need.

## R068 — Design a business proposal

### Assessment conditions

- OCR-set assignment; centre assessed and OCR moderated.
- 60 marks; normally 10–14 GLH for all assessment tasks.
- Six practical tasks.
- R068 should be completed before R069 because the same proposal is developed, marketed and pitched.
- The live assignment is time-limited and must be the correct version for the assessment series.

### Topic Area 1 / Task 1 — Complete market research to aid decisions

Current June 2026 sample assignment requirement for assessment from January 2027:

- Create and use **two** tools: one primary and one secondary.
- Use relevant qualitative and quantitative data.
- Define research aims.
- Select and apply sampling: cluster, convenience, random or quota as appropriate.
- Collate and present findings.
- Review results against the research aims.
- Reference secondary sources.
- 12 marks in the sample assignment.

Older reports refer to three tools; that is historical and must not be taught as the current task requirement.

### Topic Area 2 / Task 2 — Identify a customer profile

- Use findings rather than generic stereotypes.
- Select relevant segmentation characteristics.
- Justify the profile with market-research evidence.
- 6 marks in the sample assignment.

### Topic Area 3 / Tasks 3 and 4 — Develop a product proposal

- Design mix: function, aesthetics and economic manufacture.
- Use a creative technique to generate one design proposal under the June 2026 update.
- Explain how the design mix and research influenced the proposal.
- Conduct honest self-assessment.
- Gather verbal and written feedback.
- Review, modify and finalise the design.
- Explain decisions and how feedback was used.
- Task 3: 9 marks; Task 4: 9 marks in the sample assignment.

Older assignments requested two design ideas; the current requirement is one.

### Topic Area 4 / Task 5 — Financial viability

- Calculate realistic costs, price, sales, revenue, profit/loss and break-even.
- Use evidence from research/proposal rather than invented unsupported values.
- Interpret calculations, not merely present them.
- Evaluate whether the proposal is financially viable.
- 18 marks in the sample assignment.

### Topic Area 5 / Task 6 — Risks and likely success

- Identify relevant external risks/challenges: political, economic, social, technological, legal and environmental where applicable.
- Assess likely impact on this proposal.
- Suggest proportionate mitigation or response.
- Review likely success using the whole proposal.
- 6 marks in the sample assignment.

## R069 — Market and pitch a business proposal

### Assessment conditions

- OCR-set assignment; centre assessed and OCR moderated.
- 60 marks; normally 10–14 GLH.
- Five tasks based on the R068 proposal.
- Professional pitch must last 5–10 minutes.
- At least one independent person must witness the pitch; a teacher observation record is required.
- Practice-pitch feedback evidence is required.

### Topic Area 1 / Task 1 — Develop a brand

- Brand personality, identity and image.
- Brand purposes and methods.
- Competitor strengths/weaknesses and position.
- Unique selling point.
- External factors affecting brand choices.
- Use R068 research/customer profile.
- 12 marks in the sample assignment, presented as two six-mark grids from the June 2026 update.

### Topic Area 2 / Task 2 — Promotional campaign

- Campaign objective and key performance indicator.
- Appropriate timeframe.
- Two coherent promotional materials under the June 2026 update: one digital and one non-digital.
- Consistency with brand and customer profile.
- Explain the combination and how it communicates the proposal.
- 9 marks in the sample assignment.

Older assignments requested three materials; do not use that rule for current assessment.

### Topic Area 3 / Task 3 — Develop and practise the pitch

- Audience, venue, resources and visual aids.
- Logical structure and persuasive content.
- Anticipate questions.
- Verbal delivery, non-verbal communication and notes.
- Time control.
- Run a practice pitch, record feedback and make evidenced changes.
- 12 marks in the sample assignment.

### Topic Area 3 / Task 4 — Professional pitch

- Deliver for 5–10 minutes.
- Communicate the R068 proposal, brand and campaign clearly.
- Use multiple suitable resource types rather than slides alone.
- Respond professionally and demonstrate knowledge.
- Teacher observation contributes essential evidence.
- 15 marks in the sample assignment.

### Topic Area 4 / Task 5 — Reflect and develop

- Review the whole pitch and proposal using evidence.
- Identify what worked and what did not.
- Connect feedback to conclusions.
- Recommend realistic improvements and future development.
- 12 marks in the sample assignment, presented as two six-mark grids after the June 2026 update.

## What moderators repeatedly report

- NEA marks application and practical evidence, not pages of recalled theory. Since the redeveloped qualification, PO1 recall is not assessed in NEA.
- Students must make independent decisions. Teacher-created answer frames or extra prompts can become over-direction.
- The assignment brief cannot be altered.
- Organised evidence, page references and clearly labelled files help moderation.
- R069 presentation slides alone do not prove the full pitch or technical/persuasive performance.
- Promotional materials must be genuinely different types and coherent as a campaign.
- Practice pitch forms, final resources and observation records all matter.

## What the 2025 R067 examiner report highlights

Successful students:

- attempted all questions;
- used the enterprise context rather than generic statements;
- knew core financial calculations;
- made clear effects/consequences;
- followed the bullet prompts in the eight-mark response.

Common weaknesses:

- describing marketing-mix elements without showing interdependence;
- generic answers that could fit any business;
- confusing a questionnaire method with its online distribution;
- defining break-even when the question asks how it is used;
- vague or incomplete theory and omitted questions;
- stereotypes in customer analysis.
