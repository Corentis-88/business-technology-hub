Research snapshot: 9 July 2026  
Intended audience: the team building a student revision and planning website for Manchester Enterprise Academy (MEA)  
Scope: information gathering only. No website has been built in this phase.

## Critical findings

1. MEA’s own maps prove that Creative iMedia uses **R095 Characters and comics**. R094 is taught with Photoshop; R095 starts in Year 10 and is completed in Year 11.
2. MEA’s Computer Science map covers the whole J277 specification in Year 10, with Year 11 focused on retrieval, harder questions and alternating papers.
3. OCR changed the J837 NEA assignments for assessment from January 2027. R068 now requires one primary and one secondary research tool and one product design; R069 now requires two promotional materials, one digital and one non-digital. Older moderator reports describe older assignment versions and must not override the June 2026 specification/live assignment.
4. The published MEA Year 11 Enterprise map appears to contain copied GCSE Business material on pages 3–5. Exact Year 11 Enterprise pacing needs staff confirmation before it is shown to students.
5. The final summer 2027 timetables place Pearson Business Paper 2 and OCR Enterprise R067 in the same Friday 21 May PM session. If MEA permits students to take both subjects, this is a centre-managed clash that the exams officer must confirm.
6. Cambridge National live assignments and teacher-only candidate exemplars must never be exposed. Public sample assignments may be used for practice. The website must not provide live-NEA answers, answer templates or over-direction.

## Evidence priority

Where sources disagree, use this order:

1. current specification and live awarding-body update;
2. current official assessment material, mark scheme or timetable;
3. latest examiner/moderator report;
4. MEA’s own published curriculum map for school sequencing;
5. awarding-body video/blog/support material;
6. established teacher-created resource;
7. community discussion, used only to identify student needs.

Every time-sensitive item should show a “checked on” date in the eventual site and be reviewed at least annually.
