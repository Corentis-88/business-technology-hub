# Resource and YouTube catalogue

The detailed video list is in `youtube-catalogue.csv`. This document evaluates the wider ecosystem and explains how it should be used.

## Evaluation criteria

Rate every external resource on:

- exact qualification/code alignment;
- current specification/version;
- authority and named author/teacher;
- full-course versus selective coverage;
- opportunity for retrieval/application rather than passive watching;
- quality of answers/feedback;
- accessibility, reading load and captions;
- free, freemium, subscription or paid;
- login/data/privacy implications;
- copyright/licensing permission for linking, embedding or adapting.

Third-party material should be linked or embedded only where permitted. Its wording, diagrams, questions and model answers should not be copied into MEA content without a licence.

## Pearson Business

### High priority

- **Pearson qualification and assessment pages** — definitive specification, papers, reports and assessment support.
- **Pearson UK & International Schools YouTube** — the strongest video collection found because it is awarding-body material. The catalogue includes Theme 1/2, application, calculations, marking, nine/twelve-mark technique, revision and lessons from 2023/2024 papers.
- **BBC Bitesize Edexcel Business** — accessible topic summaries and checks at https://www.bbc.co.uk/bitesize/examspecs/z98snbk. Check each page against 1BS0 before deep-linking.

### Useful teacher-created sources

- **Two Teachers** — course videos and contemporary business examples: https://www.twoteachers.co.uk/videos
- **Bizconsesh** — concise GCSE Business topic and extended-answer videos: https://www.youtube.com/c/BizConSeshGCSEBusiness91
- **Business Teacher T** — short command-word demonstrations, including Edexcel three-mark technique.
- **BusinessEd** — topic and teaching resources: https://www.businessed.co.uk/
- **tutor2u** — Business explanations and Edexcel marking-grid summaries: https://www.tutor2u.net/business/reference/marking-grids-for-edexcel-gcse-business
- **Seneca** — specification courses and adaptive questions; account/product terms should be reviewed before recommending.

### Paid/print options

- Pearson REVISE Edexcel GCSE Business guide and workbook.
- Other current 1BS0 guides can supplement practice, but edition/code must be visible.

### Best use

Use an official Pearson explanation first for assessment rules. Use teacher channels for alternate explanations and worked examples. Pair every video with retrieval and a mark-scheme-checked question.

## OCR Enterprise and Marketing J837

### High priority

- **OCR qualification/assessment pages** — specification, public R067 papers, sample assignments, reports, candidate-style exam answers and student NEA guidance.
- **OCR June 2026 changes blog** — essential for assessment from January 2027; older resources may still say three research tools, two designs or three promotional materials.
- **Mr Weir’s Computer Science YouTube channel** — an exact R067 numbered series was found, including 1.1, 1.2 and 4.1. Check each video against the June 2026 specification.

### Paid/print options

- OCR-endorsed Hachette/Hodder student book: Tess Bayley and Leanna Oliver, ISBN 9781398351219.
- ClearRevise J837 illustrated revision and practice: https://clearrevise.com/titles/ocr-enterprise-and-marketing-j837/
- Mean Business R067 revision/retrieval products: https://www.meanbusiness.co.uk/
- TES resources vary in quality and assignment version; never assume a paid worksheet is current or permissible for live NEA.

### Gap found

There is less high-quality, free, exact-J837 video coverage than for Business or Computer Science. This is a genuine opportunity for the MEA site: complete R067 explanations, financial practice and context-rich eight-mark feedback could fill a large student need.

## OCR Computer Science J277

### High priority

- **OCR qualification/assessment pages** — definitive content, sample/public papers and reports.
- **Craig ’n’ Dave** — 88 videos mapped to every J277 learning objective: https://craigndave.org/ocr-gcse-j277-resources/
- **CSNewbs** — accessible written and visual notes following OCR J277: https://www.csnewbs.com/ocr-gcse
- **Isaac Computer Science** — GCSE topic explanations, questions and videos: https://isaaccomputerscience.org/

### Additional video/practice sources

- Mr Moore’s Paper 2 Python/algorithm tips.
- Save My Exams short J277 exam-tip and eight-mark videos.
- GCSE Computer Science Tutor and Mr Doble resources for walkthroughs/algorithms. Predictions should not be used as a curriculum filter.
- Smart Revise offers retrieval/spacing through the Craig ’n’ Dave ecosystem; cost, school licensing and evidence claims should be checked before integration.

### Textbooks and guides

OCR lists current endorsed resources at https://www.ocr.org.uk/qualifications/gcse/computer-science-j277-from-2020/textbooks/, including Hodder, Cambridge University Press and PG Online titles. ClearRevise also offers a J277 guide and exam-tutor workbook.

### Best use

Video coverage is strong; the value gap is active programming, tracing, boundary testing, immediate misconception feedback and a coherent MEA sequence. Do not replace practical coding with videos.

## Student-community findings

GCSE discussion threads repeatedly mention:

- difficulty knowing which Computer Science resource matches OCR J277;
- a perceived shortage of Creative iMedia and Enterprise revision resources;
- uncertainty about how to score on high-mark Business responses;
- a tendency to save scarce past papers or rely on predictions;
- demand for concise content plus exam practice and feedback.

These are needs signals, not evidence for assessment rules. The product response should be a clear exact-course label, complete specification tracker, abundant original practice, and source-linked advice.

## Recommended video learning pattern

1. Show the exact specification objective and a two-question pre-check.
2. Watch only the relevant segment/video.
3. Hide the video and retrieve three key ideas.
4. Answer one new application/exam question.
5. Mark using official criteria and correct the error.
6. Schedule a spaced revisit.

Do not measure “video watched” as mastery.
