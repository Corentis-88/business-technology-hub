# Method and evidence

## Research question

What must a revision and planning website know in order to support an MEA student through the exact content, assessment, exam technique, practical work and revision demands of Pearson Edexcel GCSE Business 1BS0, OCR Cambridge National Enterprise and Marketing J837, OCR GCSE Computer Science J277 and OCR Cambridge National Creative iMedia J834 with R095?

## Method

The research used five layers:

1. **Qualification identification.** MEA Year 10 and Year 11 curriculum maps were matched against awarding-body titles, unit codes and topic language.
2. **Specification mapping.** The current specification was read for every course, including all content statements, formulas, assessment objectives, unit marks, guided learning hours and administrative rules.
3. **Assessment evidence.** Sample assessment materials, recent public papers, mark schemes, candidate-style answers, examiner reports, moderator reports and assessment guides were compared.
4. **Wider teaching ecosystem.** Awarding-body videos, teacher channels, specialist revision platforms, school revision lists, community discussions and commercial resources were reviewed to find explanations, common pain points and useful delivery formats.
5. **Revision and student support.** Evidence-based study strategies, metacognitive planning, exam rules and NHS exam-stress guidance were mapped onto product requirements.

## Currency

- Research checked on **9 July 2026**.
- OCR specifications used here are the versions published in **June 2026** for J834 and J837 and **May 2026** for J277.
- The Pearson Business specification is Issue 2, July 2022; Pearson’s current assessment support page was checked in 2026.
- Final summer 2027 timetables were available and archived.
- Grade boundaries are retrospective evidence only. The latest available summer GCSE boundaries in this pack are June 2025; Cambridge National January 2026 unit boundaries are also recorded.

## Confidence labels for future content

- **Authoritative:** stated in a current specification, official mark scheme, timetable or formal awarding-body update.
- **Official guidance:** awarding-body examiner report, moderator report, blog, video or assessment-support page.
- **MEA-specific:** published by MEA and relevant to local sequencing or tools.
- **Established third party:** specialist teacher-created resource with clear specification alignment.
- **Anecdotal:** student/community advice; useful for discovering needs, never for setting rules.
- **Needs confirmation:** incomplete, inconsistent or dependent on local school choices.

## What was deliberately not done

- No live or secure Teach Cambridge assignment was downloaded or reproduced.
- No teacher-only Cambridge National candidate exemplar was copied into student material.
- No copyrighted textbook or paid revision product was reproduced.
- No prediction was made about future paper topics or grade boundaries.
- No assumption was made that a third-party answer structure is an examiner requirement.
- No website, database or search index was built in this phase.

## Known limitations

- Search engines and YouTube are not exhaustive. The catalogue prioritises exact-course resources, official material and well-established channels over duplicates.
- Some YouTube captions are automatic. Video summaries should be checked against the current specification before material is adapted.
- Secure Teach Cambridge resources may contain additional teacher support that was not accessible without school credentials.
- MEA’s current programming language is not explicitly named in the public curriculum map. Python is strongly suggested by the wider J277 ecosystem but must be confirmed locally.
- Exact internal deadlines, mock windows, lesson allocations, intervention timetable and student platform logins are school-controlled and therefore outside public research.

## Annual maintenance checklist

- Recheck all four qualification pages and specification version numbers.
- Replace exam dates when final timetables for the active cohort are issued.
- Add the newest public papers, mark schemes, examiner reports and moderator reports.
- Update historical grade-boundary charts without presenting them as forecasts.
- Check OCR subject updates for changes to assignment wording, marking grids, AI rules and the terminal rule.
- Confirm MEA’s chosen iMedia option, software and programming language with the subject leads.
- Link-check the resource and YouTube catalogues and record removals or paywall changes.
